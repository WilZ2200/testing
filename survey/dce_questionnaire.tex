\sectionheader{General Instructions}

Thank you for agreeing to participate in this study about breast cancer screening preferences.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item This survey asks about your preferences for different breast cancer screening options.
  \item There are \textbf{no right or wrong answers}---we are interested in your personal preferences.
  \item You will be shown pairs of hypothetical screening programs and asked to choose the one you prefer, or to opt out of both.
  \item Please read each question carefully and answer as honestly as possible.
  \item If you are unsure about a medical term, brief explanations are provided.
  \item The survey has four parts:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item Screening background and eligibility (5 minutes)
    \item Choice tasks---comparing screening programs (10--12 minutes)
    \item Sociodemographic questions (3--5 minutes)
    \item Debriefing (2 minutes)
  \end{enumerate}
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent\fbox{\parbox{\textwidth}{%
\textbf{Key Definitions:}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1cm,style=nextline]
  \item[Mammography] An X-ray of the breast used to detect tumors or abnormalities.
  \item[MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)] Uses magnets and radio waves to produce detailed breast images. No radiation involved.
  \item[Ultrasound] Uses sound waves to produce images of breast tissue. Often used as a supplement to mammography.
  \item[Sensitivity] The probability that a screening test correctly identifies cancer when it is present (detection rate).
  \item[False positive rate] The probability that a screening test incorrectly indicates cancer when none is present, leading to unnecessary follow-up.
\end{description}
}}

\noindent In this section, you will be presented with 12 choice scenarios. \noindent Each program is described by the following characteristics:

\vspace{0.3cm}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Characteristic} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
Screening method & The type of technology used (Mammography, MRI, or Ultrasound) \\
Screening frequency & How often you would be screened \\
Out-of-pocket cost & Your personal cost per screening visit (after insurance) \\
Sensitivity & The chance the test correctly detects cancer if present \\
False positive rate & The chance the test says cancer is present when it is not \\
Waiting time for results & How long until you receive your results \\
Pain/discomfort & The level of physical discomfort during the screening \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
